Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Innovation drives progress by introducing new ideas, technologies, or methods that solve problems, improve efficiency, and enhance experiences, shaping industries, societies, and everyday life.

Sustainability

Sustainability focuses on meeting present needs without compromising future generations, balancing environmental, economic, and social well-being through responsible resource use, conservation, and long-term planning.

Infrastructure

Infrastructure refers to the basic physical and organizational structures, such as transportation, communication, and utilities, essential for economic development, societal functioning, and quality of life.

Empowerment

Empowerment involves granting individuals or groups the authority, confidence, and resources to make decisions, take control, and create positive change in their lives and communities.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Digital transformation integrates technology into business processes, driving innovation and efficiency. It helps organizations adapt and improve customer interactions.

Green Energy

Green energy comes from renewable sources like solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal. It is environmentally friendly, reducing carbon emissions and dependence on fossil fuels.

Modern Infrastructure

Modern infrastructure includes advanced systems like smart transportation, sustainable buildings, and efficient energy grids, supporting urban development and improving quality of life.

"We have to go beyond the economy of material things and focus on the economy of human beings. Poverty is not created by poor people; poverty is created by the system that we have built around us. The poorest people in the world are the greatest untapped resource for development, and we need to build a system that recognizes and harnesses their potential."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
